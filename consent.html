Consent to Participate in Research

HRP-582 / v11102023

Title of Research Study

Explore LLM’s ability to generate novel research ideas in interdisciplinary areas

Principal Investigator

Huilian Sophie Qiu, Ph.D.

Supported By

The Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University

Key Information about this research study

The following is a short summary of this study to help you decide whether to be a part of this study. Information that is more detailed is explained later on in this form.

  • The purpose of this study is to evaluate whether large language models (LLMs) can generate high-quality research proposals in interdisciplinary fields.
  • You will be asked to review 3 anonymized research proposals and complete a short questionnaire rating each on novelty, clarity, and feasibility.
  • We expect that you will be in this research study for about 45–60 minutes total.
  • The primary potential risk of participation is a potential loss of confidentiality, which we will minimize through secure data handling.
  • There are no direct benefits, though your insights may inform future innovation tools.

Why am I being asked to take part in this research study?

We are asking you to take part in this research study because you are a researcher with expertise in relevant fields. You must be 18 or older and fluent in English.

How many people will be in this study?

We expect about 15 people will be in this research study.

What should I know about participating in a research study?

  • Whether or not you take part is up to you.
  • You can choose not to take part.
  • You can agree to take part and later change your mind.
  • Your decision will not be held against you.
  • You can ask all the questions you want before you decide.
  • You do not have to answer any question you do not want to answer.

What happens if I say, ‘Yes, I want to be in this research’?

  • Read and evaluate 3 anonymized research proposals via a secure online platform.
  • Complete a brief questionnaire (Likert scale + optional comments) after each proposal.
  • Your responses will be anonymized and used solely for research purposes. Total time commitment is approximately 45–60 minutes. All activities are conducted online.

Will being in this study help me in any way?

We cannot promise any benefits to you or others from your taking part in this research. There are no direct benefits to you. The study aims to inform the responsible and creative use of AI in science.

Is there any way being in this study could be bad for me? (Risks)

A possible risk for any research is that confidentiality could be compromised—that is, that people outside the study might get hold of confidential study information. We will do everything we can to minimize this risk.

What happens if I do not want to be in this research, or I change my mind later?

Participation in research is voluntary. You can decide to participate or not to participate. If you decide to withdraw from this study, the researchers will ask you if the information already collected from you can be used.

How will the researchers protect my information?

All responses will be collected via a secure, password-protected platform. Identifiers will be stored separately from study data. Only the research team will have access. Data will be stored for 3 years and then securely deleted.

Who will have access to the information collected during this research study?

Efforts will be made to limit the use and disclosure of your personal information, including research study records, to people who have a need to review this information. We cannot promise complete secrecy.

There are reasons why information about you may be used or seen by other people beyond the research team during or after this study, such as university officials, government officials, study funders, auditors, and the Institutional Review Board.

How might the information collected in this study be shared in the future?

We will keep the information we collect about you during this research study for study recordkeeping and for potential use in future research projects. De-identified data from this study may be shared with the research community, journals, and data repositories.

Will I be paid or given anything for taking part in this study?

There is no payment or reimbursement for participating in this study.
